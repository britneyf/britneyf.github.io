My goal is to build intelligent, intuitive tools at the intersection of AI, biology, and people.

Lately, I've been working on tumor plasticity and cell state transitions at MSKCC, where I design scalable single-cell RNA-seq pipelines, experiment with machine learning on patient data, and try to understand what makes cancer so clever.

I previously studied Biomedical Engineering and Mathematics at Penn State, and recently completed a master’s in Biomedical Engineering (with a Data Science focus) at Columbia. Over the years, I've worked on image segmentation with CNNs, built generative models to track wound healing, and explored how eye movements reveal what matters to clinicians.

I love thoughtful design, clear communication, and anything that brings beauty and insight to complex problems.

Some things I’ve built or contributed to:

- 🧠 [Predicting clinician eye movements with CNNs](https://doi.org/10.1109/OJEMB.2024.3367492)
- 🧬 [Single-cell RNA-seq pipelines for tumor state discovery](https://doi.org/10.1158/1538-7445.AM2025-2648)
- 🧪 A generative model to forecast wound healing from scRNA-seq
- 🩻 A 3D CNN for hemorrhagic stroke prediction from MRI scans
- 🧫 Lab-on-a-chip devices for rapid pathogen detection

I also give talks, mentor younger researchers, and run on iced coffee and curiosity.

If you’re in NYC and want to chat about AI, cancer biology, art, or weird internet subcultures — let’s connect.

> **Currently:** exploring how to make biomedical research workflows more beautiful, reproducible, and human-friendly.
